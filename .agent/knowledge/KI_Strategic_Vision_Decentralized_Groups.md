# KI: Strategic Vision: Decentralized Moqui Groups

## Context
The "moqui-ai" project is more than just a toolset; it is a framework for building a **Decentralized Intelligence/Enterprise Network**.

## Core Objective
To enable independent **Moqui Groups** (associations of moqui-ai users) to collaborate on the creation, marketing, and maintenance of high-quality business products (like `aitree`) while maintaining absolute sovereignty.

## Principles of Independence
1. **No Central Corporate Pressure**: The architecture must avoid a single central point of failure or control. Unlike cloud-first platforms (e.g., Google or EU-regulated entities), a Moqui Group's data, models, and logic should reside on their own hardware.
2. **Sovereignty**: Each Moqui Group must be able to operate independently of any other group or central "parent" entity if necessary. 

## The "Group-Based" Collaboration Model
*   **Intra-Group Sharing**: High-trust sharing of models, fine-tuning adapters (LoRAs), and detailed clinical/business-specific Knowledge Items (KIs).
*   **Inter-Group Sharing (The "Outside" Layer)**: Selective, anonymized sharing of framework-level improvements, general AI blueprints, and common Moqui logic. 

## Technical Requirements for Independence
*   **Local LLM (Ollama/Qwen)**: The "brain" must be local to stay outside the reach of "central corporate" oversight or API censorship.
*   **Portable Knowledge (.agent/knowledge)**: Knowledge must be stored as plain, version-controlled text (Markdown) that can be moved or shared via peer-to-peer (P2P) or private Git networks.
*   **Decentralized Intelligence**: Eventually implement "Federated Learning" or "Knowledge Registries" so that Moqui Groups can learn from each other without ever needing a central "Authority."

## The Vision
We are creating a structure where a thousand small, independent Moqui Groups are more resilient and more innovative than one giant, regulated corporation.
